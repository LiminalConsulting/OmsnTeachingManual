## 3.1 Energy Transformations and Transfers

### Fundamental Types of Energy

**Exercise 1.** *(Manual: Kinetic and Potential Energy)*

a) What is kinetic energy? What quantities does it depend on?

b) What is potential energy? Name four types.

c) What is gravitational potential energy? What quantities does it depend on?

---

**Exercise 2.** *(Manual: Kinetic Energy)*

Calculate the kinetic energy in the following cases (Ek = m/2 x v^2):

a) A 1000 kg car at 20 m/s.

b) A 0.5 kg ball at 10 m/s.

c) A 70 kg cyclist at 5 m/s.

---

**Exercise 3.** *(Manual: Gravitational Potential Energy)*

Calculate the gravitational potential energy in the following cases (Ep = m x g x h, g = 9.8 N/kg):

a) A 2 kg book at 1.5 m height.

b) A 0.4 kg ball at 10 m height.

c) A 60 kg person at the top of a 3 m ladder.

---

### Energy Transformations

**Exercise 4.** *(Manual: Energy Transformations)*

a) What happens to potential energy and kinetic energy during a falling body?

b) And during a rise?

c) State the principle of conservation of mechanical energy in the absence of air resistance.

---

**Exercise 5.** *(Manual: Mechanical Energy)*

A 0.5 kg ball is thrown vertically upward from the ground with a velocity of 10 m/s.

a) Calculate the kinetic energy at the moment of launch.

b) What is the gravitational potential energy at ground level?

c) Calculate the total mechanical energy.

d) At the highest point, what is the kinetic energy? And the potential energy?
