## 2.1 Movements on Earth

### Position and Movement of a Body

**Exercise 1.** *(Manual: Reference Frame)*

a) What is a reference frame?

b) When is a body said to be at rest?

c) When is a body said to be in motion?

d) Is a passenger sitting on a moving train at rest or in motion? Justify considering different reference frames.

---

**Exercise 2.** *(Manual: Trajectory and Distance Traveled)*

a) Distinguish between rectilinear and curvilinear trajectory.

b) A car travels along a straight road. The initial position is xi = 2 km and the final position is xf = 17 km. Calculate the distance traveled.

c) A cyclist starts at position xi = 10 m and arrives at position xf = 85 m. Calculate the distance traveled.

---

### Speed and Velocity

**Exercise 3.** *(Manual: Average Speed)*

Calculate the average speed in the following cases:

a) A runner covers 100 m in 12.5 s.

b) A car covers 150 km in 2 hours.

c) A bicycle covers 30 km in 1.5 hours.

---

**Exercise 4.** *(Manual: Velocity)*

a) What is the difference between speed and velocity?

b) Why is velocity called a vector quantity?

c) Convert the following speeds to m/s:

   i) 72 km/h

   ii) 36 km/h

   iii) 108 km/h

---

### Acceleration

**Exercise 5.** *(Manual: Average Acceleration)*

Calculate the average acceleration in the following cases:

a) A car goes from 0 m/s to 20 m/s in 10 s.

b) A cyclist slows down from 15 m/s to 5 m/s in 4 s.

c) An airplane accelerates from 50 m/s to 80 m/s in 6 s.

---

**Exercise 6.** *(Manual: Movement Types)*

Classify the following movements as uniform, uniformly accelerated, or uniformly decelerated:

a) Constant velocity of 30 m/s.

b) Velocity increasing from 0 to 20 m/s at a constant rate.

c) Velocity decreasing from 25 m/s to 0 at a constant rate.

d) A car at 90 km/h on a highway without changing speed.

---

### Safety Distance

**Exercise 7.** *(Manual: Safety Distance)*

A car travels at 20 m/s. The driver's reaction time is 1 s and the braking time is 3 s.

a) Calculate the reaction distance.

b) Calculate the braking distance.

c) Calculate the total safety distance.

---

**Exercise 8.** *(Manual: Influencing Factors)*

a) Name three factors that increase reaction time.

b) Name three factors that affect braking distance.

c) If reaction time doubles, what happens to the safety distance?

---

### Position-Time Graphs

**Exercise 9.** *(Manual: Position-Time Graphs)*

On a position-time graph:

a) What does a horizontal line indicate?

b) What does a steeper slope indicate?

c) A body is at position 10 m at 0 s and at position 50 m at 8 s, moving in a straight line. Calculate the average speed.

## 2.2 Forces and Movements

### Forces

**Exercise 10.** *(Manual: Force)*

a) What is a force? What are its four characteristics?

b) Which law states: "For every action there is a reaction with the same magnitude, the same direction, but opposite sense"?

c) Give an example of an action-reaction pair.

---

**Exercise 11.** *(Manual: Resultant Force)*

Calculate the resultant force in the following cases:

a) Two forces of 10 N and 6 N in the same direction and sense.

b) Two forces of 15 N and 8 N in the same direction, opposite senses.

c) Two perpendicular forces of 3 N and 4 N.

---

### Newton's Laws

**Exercise 12.** *(Manual: Newton's 1st Law)*

a) State the Law of Inertia (Newton's 1st Law).

b) What is inertia? What does it depend on?

c) Why should seat belts be worn? Answer using the concept of inertia.

---

**Exercise 13.** *(Manual: Newton's 2nd Law)*

Use the formula Fr = m x a to solve:

a) Calculate the acceleration of a 5 kg body subject to a resultant force of 20 N.

b) Calculate the resultant force needed to accelerate a 1000 kg car at 2 m/s^2.

c) Calculate the mass of a body that undergoes an acceleration of 3 m/s^2 when a force of 15 N is applied.

---

**Exercise 14.** *(Manual: Pressure)*

Calculate the pressure in the following cases:

a) A force of 500 N applied to an area of 0.5 m^2.

b) A force of 800 N applied to an area of 0.02 m^2.

c) Explain why high-heeled shoes leave marks on the floor, but flat shoes do not.

---

d) How can friction be reduced?
