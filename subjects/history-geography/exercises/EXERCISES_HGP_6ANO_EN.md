## Answers

*(For verification after completing the exercises)*

**6. Portugal in the 13th and 14th Centuries**
- Ex 1: Nobility: Yes / Fighting / No; Clergy: Yes / Religious service / No; People: No / Working the land / Yes
- Ex 2: a) T, b) F (six days a week), c) T, d) F (that was the peasants' diet), e) T
- Ex 3: a) Secular clergy live among the population; regular clergy live in monasteries, b) Teaching, copying books, tending the sick, farming, c) Monks who copied books; illuminations are paintings illustrating the text
- Ex 4: a) foral / charter, b) privileges, c) *homens-bons* (good men), d) town / surrounding territory
- Ex 5: a) Internal: within the country; External: between countries, b) Larger than markets, more products; developed internal trade, c) Common men (merchants and artisans) who enriched themselves through external trade

**7. Crisis of 1383–1385**
- Ex 6: 1) Famine, 2) Epidemics/Black Death, 3) Wars
- Ex 7: a) 1347, b) About one third, c) Rat fleas and through the air, d) 1348
- Ex 8: a) D, b) S, c) P, d) E, e) S, f) E
- Ex 9: Dona Leonor de Teles, Dona Beatriz, foreign
- Ex 10: Upper Clergy/high Nobility — a); People/bourgeoisie — b)
- Ex 11: 4, 1, 3, 5, 2
- Ex 12: a) Nuno Álvares Pereira, b) England; through the marriage of King João I to Dona Filipa de Lencastre (1387), c) A chronicler who recorded what happened in this period

**8. Portugal in the 15th and 16th Centuries**
- Ex 13: a) Wealth and new markets, b) Titles and lands, c) Convert other peoples to Christianity, d) Better living conditions
- Ex 14: 1415-c, 1419-e, 1434-a, 1488-d, 1498-f, 1500-b
- Ex 15: a) caravel / tack (sail against the wind), b) quadrant / astrolabe / cross-staff, c) nautical charts
- Ex 16: a) According to the Treaty of Alcáçovas, lands south of the Canaries belonged to Portugal, b) Division of the world by a meridian 370 leagues west of Cape Verde, c) Reaching India by sea
- Ex 17: Orient: spices, silks, porcelain, precious stones; Africa: gold, malagueta pepper, ivory, slaves; Brazil: sugar, brazilwood, exotic animals
- Ex 18: a) T, b) T, c) F (that was where merchants were; the *Ribeira das Naus* was where ships were built), d) T, e) F (most lived in extreme poverty)
- Ex 19: a) He died at the Battle of Alcácer-Quibir without heirs, causing a succession problem, b) Philip II of Spain (clergy/nobility/high bourgeoisie), Dom António (people/part of nobility), Dona Catarina (nobles/clergy)
