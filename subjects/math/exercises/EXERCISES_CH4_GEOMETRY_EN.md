## 4.1 Basic Elements

### 4.1.1 Angles

**Exercise 1.** *(Manual: Basic Elements - Classification of Angles)*

Classify the following angles as acute, right, obtuse, straight, or full rotation:

a) 45 degrees

b) 90 degrees

c) 135 degrees

d) 180 degrees

e) 360 degrees

---

**Exercise 2.** *(Manual: Basic Elements - Pairs of Angles)*

a) Two angles are complementary. One of them measures 35 degrees. How much does the other measure?

b) Two angles are supplementary. One of them measures 110 degrees. How much does the other measure?

c) Two angles are vertically opposite. One measures 72 degrees. How much does the other measure?

---

**Exercise 3.** *(Manual: Basic Elements - Angle Measurement)*

Convert:

a) 2 degrees and 30 minutes into minutes

b) 150 minutes into degrees and minutes

c) 1 degree, 15 minutes and 30 seconds into seconds

---

### 4.1.2 Parallelism and Perpendicularity

**Exercise 4.** *(Manual: Basic Elements - Parallelism and Perpendicularity)*

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One of the angles formed measures 65 degrees.

a) What is the measure of the alternate interior angle?

b) What is the measure of the corresponding angle?

c) What is the measure of the supplementary adjacent angle?

---

### BONUS - 4.1 Basic Elements

## 4.2 Plane Figures

### 4.2.1 Triangles

**Exercise 5.** *(Manual: Plane Figures - Triangles)*

Classify the following triangles by sides and by angles:

a) Triangle with sides 5 cm, 5 cm, 5 cm and three 60-degree angles

b) Triangle with sides 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm and one 90-degree angle

c) Triangle with sides 6 cm, 6 cm, 4 cm and all acute angles

---

**Exercise 6.** *(Manual: Plane Figures - Triangles - Sum of Interior Angles)*

In a triangle, two angles measure 45 degrees and 70 degrees. How much does the third angle measure?

---

**Exercise 7.** *(Manual: Plane Figures - Triangles - Sum of Interior Angles)*

---

**Exercise 8.** *(Manual: Plane Figures - Triangles - Triangle Inequality)*

a) 3, 4, 5

b) 1, 2, 5

c) 7, 7, 12

**Exercise 9.** *(Manual: Plane Figures - Congruence Criteria)*

Two triangles have the following data. State the congruence criterion that applies (SSS, SAS, or ASA):

a) Three sides: 3 cm, 5 cm and 7 cm in both triangles

b) Two sides of 4 cm and 6 cm with the angle between them of 60 degrees in both

c) Two angles of 50 degrees and 70 degrees with the side between them of 5 cm in both

## 4.3 Geometric Transformations

b) A figure "slides" in a direction without rotating

**Exercise 17.** *(Manual: Geometric Transformations - Axial Reflection)*

### 4.3.2 Similarities

**Exercise 18.** *(Manual: Geometric Transformations - Similarities)*

Two similar triangles have a similarity ratio r = 2. The smaller triangle has sides of 3 cm, 4 cm and 5 cm.

a) What are the sides of the larger triangle?

b) If the perimeter of the smaller triangle is 12 cm, what is the perimeter of the larger triangle?

c) If the area of the smaller triangle is 6 cm^2, what is the area of the larger triangle?

---

**Exercise 19.** *(Manual: Geometric Transformations - Similarity Criteria)*

b) Two triangles with proportional sides: 3, 6, 9 and 1, 2, 3

c) Two triangles with two proportional sides (3, 6 and 1, 2) and the angle between them equal
