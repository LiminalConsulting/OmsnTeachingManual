## 3.4 Functions

*(For verification after completing the exercises)*

**3.1 Algebraic Expressions**
- Ex 1: a) 5x, 3y, -7; b) 5, 3, -7; c) x, y, no literal part
- Ex 2: a) 3n + 4; b) x/2 - 3; c) a^2 + a
- Ex 3: a) -4; b) 15; c) 21
- Ex 4: a) 5a; b) 4x + 7; c) m + 8n - 4
- Ex 5: a) 5x + 6; b) 3a - 11; c) 5y + 10
- Ex 6: a) 6x + 15; b) -8a + 6; c) 8x - 2
- Ex 7: a) commutative; b) associative; c) distributive; d) identity element
- B1: 6x + 4
- B2: Yes, they are equivalent

**3.2 Sequences and Patterns**
- Ex 8: a) 14, 17; b) 25, 36; c) 48, 96
- Ex 9: a) 5; b) 40; c) 100
- Ex 10: a) a_n = 2n; b) a_n = 3n + 2; c) a_n = 3(n - 1) = 3n - 3
- Ex 11: a) 4, 7, 10, 13, 16; b) 31; c) Yes (n = 15)
- Ex 12: a) 9 dots; b) a_n = 2n - 1; c) 99 dots
- B3: a) each term is the sum of the two previous terms (Fibonacci); b) 13, 21, 34, 55

**3.3 Equations and Inequalities**
- Ex 13: a) x = 7; b) x = 7; c) x = 7; d) x = 5
- Ex 14: a) x = 5; b) x = 5; c) x = 13/2 = 6.5
- Ex 15: a) indeterminate possible; b) impossible; c) determinate possible (x = 2)
- Ex 16: Maria = 6 years; Joao = 18 years
- Ex 17: a) x = 7 or x = -7; b) x = 4 or x = -4; c) x = 5 or x = -5
- Ex 18: a) x = 2 or x = 3; b) x = 2 or x = -5; c) x = 3 or x = 1/2
- Ex 19: a) x > 4; b) x < 7; c) x >= 3
- Ex 20: a) x < -3; b) x >= -3; c) x < 8
- B4: 10 cars and 8 motorcycles
- B5: width = 6 cm, length = 8 cm

**3.4 Functions**
- Ex 21: a) yes; b) no (a number can have several divisors); c) yes

- Ex 28: a) t = 120/v; b) 2 hours; c) 1.5 hours; d) inverse
- B6: After 5 km
- B7: t = 4 seconds
