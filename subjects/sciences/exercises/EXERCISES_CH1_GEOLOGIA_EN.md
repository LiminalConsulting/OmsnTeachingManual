## Answers

*(For verification after completing the exercises)*

**1.1 Landscapes and Earth's Structure**
- Ex 1: a) volcanic igneous, b) detrital sedimentary, c) plutonic igneous, d) chemical sedimentary, e) metamorphic
- Ex 2: a) North and center, b) Azores and Madeira, c) Western and southern coasts, Tagus and Sado basins
- Ex 3: a) Landscape whose predominant element is rock, b) Portion of rock emerging from the soil surface
- Ex 4: a) D, b) I, c) D, d) I, e) D, f) I
- Ex 5: Geochemical - chemical composition - Crust, Mantle, Core; Geophysical - physical properties - Lithosphere, Asthenosphere, Mesosphere, Endosphere
- Ex 6: a) GC, b) GP, c) GC, d) GP, e) GC
- Ex 7: 1-c, 2-d, 3-a, 4-b
- Ex 8: a) photosynthesis (CO2/O2), b) erosive action of water on rocks, c) volcanic gas emissions
- B1: Recreated primitive Earth conditions and produced organic compounds without living organisms

**1.2 Rocks and Minerals**
- Ex 9: a) igneous, b) sedimentary, c) metamorphic, d) igneous, e) sedimentary, f) metamorphic
- Ex 10: a) luster, b) streak, c) hardness, d) cleavage, e) color
- Ex 11: a) T, b) F (each mineral has a defined chemical composition), c) F (composed of various minerals), d) T, e) F (cleavage = flat surfaces; fracture = irregular)
- Ex 12: a) wind, b) water, c) living organisms, d) water
- Ex 13: d) weathering, b) erosion, f) transport, c) sedimentation, e) compaction, a) cementation
- Ex 14: a) D, b) C, c) B, d) D, e) B, f) C
- Ex 15: Plutonic - depth/slow - visible - Granite; Volcanic - surface/rapid - agranular - Basalt; Contact - intrusions - foliated - Marble; Regional - convergent - foliated - Schist
- Ex 16: a) cooling, b) metamorphism, c) melting, d) sedimentogenesis and diagenesis
- B2: Open answer referring to continuous cycle of formation, transformation, and destruction

**1.3 Earth's Dynamics**
- Ex 17: a) Alfred Wegener, b) single supercontinent, c) single ocean surrounding Pangaea
- Ex 18: a) M, b) P, c) G, d) PC
- Ex 19: a) mountain chain in the middle of the ocean floor, b) central valley of the ridge where magma is expelled, c) deep elongated depression, d) relatively flat shallow surface near continents
- Ex 20: Divergent - move apart - lithosphere formation - volcanism and seismicity; Convergent - collide - subduction or mountain formation - volcanism and seismicity; Transform - slide past - no formation/destruction - high seismicity
- Ex 21: a) F (compressive), b) T, c) F (extensional), d) T, e) T
- Ex 22: a) magma chamber, b) chimney/conduit, c) crater, d) volcanic cone
- Ex 23: Explosive - silica/viscous/many/tall; Effusive - silica/fluid/few/low
- Ex 24: a) S, b) L, c) G, d) S, e) G, f) S
- Ex 25: a) hypocenter = internal location where energy is released; epicenter = surface point directly above hypocenter, b) Richter measures magnitude; EMS measures intensity, c) magnitude = energy released; intensity = effects felt
- Ex 26: a) know where to shut off water/gas/electricity, have battery radio, water and food reserves; b) get under table, cover head, stay calm; c) shut off water/gas/electricity, don't light matches, listen to radio
- B3: Open answer referring to plate boundaries and convection currents

**1.4 Earth's History**
- Ex 27: a) T, b) B, c) T, d) B, e) T, f) B
- Ex 28: presence of skeleton, rapid burial, lack of oxygen (or low temperature, absence of decomposers)
- Ex 29: Index fossils: short existence period, wide distribution (e.g., trilobites); Facies fossils: indicate specific habitats (e.g., corals)
- Ex 30: Principle of superposition (lower strata are older) and Principle of horizontality (sediments deposit in horizontal layers)
- Ex 31: 1-b, 2-d, 3-a, 4-c
- B4: Historical time refers to human history (years/centuries); geological time refers to Earth's history (millions of years). Age of Earth: approximately 4,600 million years.

**1.5 Geological Sustainability**
- Ex 32: E.g., slate/marble/granite for construction; sand for glass; clay for ceramics
- Ex 33: Negative: landscape destruction, pollution. Measures: reduce waste, recycle materials
- Ex 34: E.g., iodine deficiency affects thyroid; radon gas accumulation in buildings in uranium-rich regions
- B5: Open answer about analysis of geological substrate, soil permeability, aquifer contamination
