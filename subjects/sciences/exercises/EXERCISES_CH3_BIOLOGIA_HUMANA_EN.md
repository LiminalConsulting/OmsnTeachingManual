## Answers

*(For verification after completing the exercises)*

**3.1 Health and Body Organization**
- Ex 1: A state of physical, mental, and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity
- Ex 2: a) C, b) B, c) P, d) C, e) B, f) P
- Ex 3: a) fecal-oral route, b) airborne route, c) vectors, d) blood-borne route
- Ex 4: a) F (only with medical prescription), b) T, c) T, d) F (bacterial infections), e) F (never share)
- Ex 5: Smoking, sedentary lifestyle, poor diet, alcoholism (or obesity, chronic stress)
- Ex 6: Balanced diet, regular exercise, not smoking, sleeping 7-9 hours
- Ex 7: d, b, e, a, c
- B1: Biological: genetics, age; Behavioral: diet, physical activity

**3.2 Nutrition and Digestion**
- Ex 8: Proteins - structural/plastic; Carbohydrates - energy; Lipids - energy/heat; Vitamins - regulatory; Minerals - regulatory; Water - regulatory/plastic
- Ex 9: a) O/MA, b) I/MA, c) O/MI, d) O/MA, e) I/MI, f) O/MA
- Ex 10: 1-b-iii, 2-a-ii, 3-c-i
- Ex 11: a) abnormal body perception with strict dieting; b) excessive eating followed by vomiting; c) uncontrolled eating
- Ex 12: Mouth, pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum, anus
- Ex 13: Salivary glands (saliva), liver (bile), pancreas (pancreatic juice)
- Ex 14: Mouth - chewing - maltose - bolus; Stomach - peristaltic - peptides - chyme; Duodenum - bile - chyle
- Ex 15: a) F (small intestine), b) T, c) T, d) T, e) F (increase)
- Ex 16: Peptic ulcer (excess acid); Colon cancer (smoking, low-fiber diet)
- B2: Synthesize amino acids/vitamins, decompose undigested nutrients, protect against pathogens

**3.3 Circulatory and Immune Systems**
- Ex 17: 1-c, 2-d, 3-b, 4-a
- Ex 18: a) 4 (A, B, AB, O), b) AB, c) O, d) No
- Ex 19: a) atria, b) ventricles, c) pericardium, d) myocardium, e) septum
- Ex 20: a) general relaxation, blood fills atria; b) atrial contraction, blood to ventricles; c) ventricular contraction, blood to arteries
- Ex 21: Arteries - thick/high; Veins - valves/low/backflow; Capillaries - substance
- Ex 22: Systemic: O2-rich blood from heart to organs; Pulmonary: O2-poor blood from heart to lungs
- Ex 23: Drainage, defense, nutrient absorption, transport
- B3: Atherosclerosis, stroke, heart attack. Prevention: don't smoke, exercise, healthy diet, manage stress

**3.4 Respiratory System**
- Ex 24: Nasal cavities, pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi, bronchioles, alveoli
- Ex 25: Inhalation - contract/descends/increases/decreases; Exhalation - relax/rises/decreases/increases
- Ex 26: Pulmonary: O2 enters blood, CO2 exits in alveoli; Tissue: O2 exits to cells, CO2 enters blood
- Ex 27: Pulmonary emphysema (smoking), lung cancer (smoking)
- B4: 1) Call 112; 2) Basic resuscitation maneuvers; 3) Defibrillation; 4) Post-resuscitation care

**3.5 Excretory System**
- Ex 28: Kidneys (filter blood), ureters (transport urine), bladder (store urine), urethra (expel urine)
- Ex 29: b, c, a
- Ex 30: a) F (proteins cannot pass due to size), b) T, c) T, d) F (decreases concentration)
- Ex 31: Excretion, thermoregulation, sensory reception (protection)
- Ex 32: Epidermis: keratin cells, melanin. Dermis: blood vessels, sweat glands
- B5: Hemodialysis: artificial blood filtration; Transplant: replacement with healthy kidney. Necessary in kidney failure

**3.6 Coordination and Regulation**
- Ex 33: CNS (brain, spinal cord), PNS (nerves, nerve ganglia)
- Ex 34: a) cell body, b) dendrites, c) axon, d) myelin sheath
- Ex 35: 1-b, 2-c, 3-a
- Ex 36: Voluntary: depends on will, cerebrum; Reflex: independent of will, spinal cord
- Ex 37: 1-b-v, 2-d-iii, 3-e-ii, 4-a-iv, 5-c-i
- Ex 38: a) pancreas produces little/no insulin; b) thyroid produces insufficient hormones; c) excess growth hormone
- B6: The hypothalamus produces stimulatory/inhibitory hormones for the pituitary, intervening in both nervous and hormonal regulation

**3.7 Reproduction and Heredity**
- Ex 39: Primary: reproductive organs present since birth (e.g., testes, ovaries); Secondary: appear during puberty (e.g., body hair growth, breast development)
- Ex 40: a) produce sperm and testosterone, b) sperm maturation and storage, c) transport sperm, d) produces prostatic fluid
- Ex 41: a) produce oocytes and hormones, b) site of fertilization, c) embryo implantation and development, d) receives sperm and birth canal
- Ex 42: Follicular - 14 - follicle; Ovulation - 14 - oocyte; Luteal - corpus luteum
- Ex 43: c, d, a, e, b
- Ex 44: 1) Dilation, 2) Expulsion of the fetus, 3) Expulsion of the placenta
- B7: Ovulation on day 14; sperm viable up to 5 days; oocyte viable 24-72h. Fertile window: 5 days before to 3 days after ovulation
