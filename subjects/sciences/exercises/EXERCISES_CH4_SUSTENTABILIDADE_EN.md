## Answers

*(For verification after completing the exercises)*

**4.1 Sustainable Development**
- Ex 1: Development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs
- Ex 2: a) Production, b) Cultural, c) Regulation, d) Support
- Ex 3: Security, basic material resources, health, social and cultural relations
- Ex 4: a) R, b) NR, c) R, d) NR, e) R, f) NR, g) NR, h) R
- Ex 5: a) water, b) mineral, c) biological, d) energy, e) biological, f) mineral
- Ex 6: a) T, b) F (non-renewable), c) T, d) F (renewable resource), e) T
- B1: Open answer: support services (soil formation, nutrient cycles) are the foundation for production, regulation, and cultural services

**4.2 Environmental Protection**
- Ex 7: a) NG, b) A, c) NC, d) NG, e) A, f) NC, g) A, h) NC
- Ex 8: 1-b, 2-a
- Ex 9: Increased erosion, pollutant dispersal, reduced biodiversity
- Ex 10: Earthquake-resistant construction, public awareness, forest preservation (or emergency plans, alert systems)
- Ex 11: a) League for the Protection of Nature, b) Fund for the Protection of Wild Animals, c) Portuguese Society for the Study of Birds, d) Group for Studies on Territorial Planning and the Environment
- Ex 12: 1-b-iii, 2-d-i, 3-c-iv, 4-a-ii
- Ex 13: a) Institute for Nature Conservation and Forests, b) EU ecological network for conservation, c) ensure long-term conservation of threatened species and habitats
- B2: Open answer: toxic substances accumulate in tissues and concentrate at higher trophic levels

**4.3 Territory and Resource Management**
- Ex 14: Allows land use planning, natural area protection, disaster prevention, and sustainability
- Ex 15: a) T, b) F (has great impact), c) T, d) F (it is essential to reduce)
- Ex 16: Turn off tap while brushing teeth, take short showers, reuse water (or fix leaks, run full washing machine loads)
- Ex 17: Treat wastewater, reduce pesticide use in agriculture
- B3: Open answer with concrete measures

**General Review**
- Ex 18: Geology - continuous rock transformation; Ecology - biotic/abiotic relationships maintain balance; Human Biology - body systems maintain internal balance; Sustainability - meeting needs without compromising the future
- Ex 19: Open answer relating geological knowledge (resources), biological knowledge (ecosystems), and sustainability (responsible management)
- B4: Open answer adapted to the student's region
