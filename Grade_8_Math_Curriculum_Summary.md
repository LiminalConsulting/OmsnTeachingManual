# Portuguese Mathematics Curriculum - Grade 8 Summary

## Mathematical Capacities (Transversal)

### Problem Solving
- **Process**: Recognize and apply problem-solving stages (interpret, select strategy, execute, evaluate)
- **Strategies**: Apply diverse strategies including simulation, working backwards, trial and error, simplifying problems, using particular cases, creating diagrams
- **Evaluation**: Recognize correctness, differences, and efficacy of different problem-solving strategies

### Mathematical Reasoning
- **Conjecturing**: Formulate and test conjectures/generalizations from identifying regularities
- **Classification**: Classify objects by mathematical characteristics
- **Justification**: Distinguish between testing and validating conjectures; justify using progressive symbolic language; recognize different forms of justification

### Computational Thinking
- **Abstraction**: Extract essential information from problems
- **Decomposition**: Structure problem resolution into smaller complexity steps
- **Pattern Recognition**: Recognize patterns and regularities to apply in similar problems
- **Algorithms**: Develop step-by-step procedures for problem solving
- **Debugging**: Search for and correct errors, test, refine and optimize solutions

### Mathematical Communication
- **Expression**: Describe mathematical thinking orally and in writing
- **Discussion**: Listen to others, question and discuss ideas fundamentally, present arguments

### Mathematical Representations
- **Multiple Representations**: Read, interpret and use diverse representations (verbal, visual, physical, contextual, symbolic)
- **Representation Connections**: Establish relationships and conversions between different representations
- **Symbolic Language**: Use mathematical symbolic language recognizing its communication value

### Mathematical Connections
- **Internal Connections**: Recognize and use connections between different mathematical themes
- **External Connections**: Apply mathematical ideas to solve problems in diverse contexts
- **Mathematical Models**: Interpret situations mathematically, construct adequate mathematical models

## Numbers

### Rational Numbers
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Representations of rational numbers
- Multiplication and division
- Powers with rational base and integer exponent
- Numerical expressions
- Mental calculation
- Square root
- Cube root
- Scientific notation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize rational numbers as finite or infinite periodic decimals
- Recognize difference between approximate and exact values
- Multiply and divide rational numbers
- Recognize properties of multiplication and division
- Understand meaning of powers with rational base and integer exponent
- Apply operational rules for powers
- Simplify and calculate expressions involving powers
- Compare and order powers
- Write, simplify and calculate numerical expressions using properties
- Use mental calculation strategies fluently
- Know perfect squares up to 144 and relate to pictorial representation
- Estimate and frame square roots using technology
- Calculate square roots of perfect squares and approximate values
- Know perfect cubes up to 125
- Solve problems involving cube roots
- Analyze real-life situations with numbers very close to zero
- Represent and compare positive rational numbers in scientific notation
- Operate with numbers in scientific notation in simple cases

**Key Skills:**
- Fluent calculation with rational numbers
- Critical use of estimates and approximate values
- Recognition of operational properties
- Use of technology for calculations
- Problem solving in diverse contexts

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate understanding of rational number representations
- Apply operations correctly in mathematical and real contexts
- Use appropriate calculation strategies
- Solve problems requiring exact or approximate solutions

## Algebra

### Algebraic Expressions and Equations
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Polynomials
- Operations with polynomials
- Solving 1st degree equations with one unknown
- Literal equations
- Systems of two 1st degree equations with two unknowns

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify monomials and polynomials
- Describe number properties and operations using polynomials
- Add and multiply polynomials
- Recognize 1st degree equations with denominators and parentheses
- Solve 1st degree equations with denominators and parentheses
- Represent situations through equations and vice versa
- Recognize formulas from other scientific areas as literal equations
- Solve 1st degree equations with two unknowns in terms of one variable
- Recognize systems of two 1st degree equations with two unknowns
- Verify algebraically or geometrically if ordered pairs are system solutions
- Solve systems using different representations, relating algebraic and geometric resolution
- Solve problems involving equation systems in diverse contexts

**Key Skills:**
- Symbolic manipulation of algebraic expressions
- Translation between verbal and algebraic representations
- Problem modeling through equations and systems
- Strategic selection of solution methods
- Connection between algebraic and geometric approaches

### Functions
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Affine functions

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize affine function as f(x) = ax + b and linear function as particular case
- Represent affine functions using multiple representations (graph, algebraic expression, table)
- Recognize effect of parameter variation in affine functions
- Interpret and model real situations with affine functions
- Identify proportional direct function with linear function
- Discuss whether affine functions are direct proportionality functions
- Model real situations through affine functions

**Key Skills:**
- Multiple representation usage and conversion
- Function parameter analysis
- Real situation modeling
- Connection between mathematical and contextual representations

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Solve algebraic equations and systems correctly
- Represent real situations algebraically
- Interpret and create function representations
- Apply functions to model and solve problems

## Data and Probability

### Statistical Questions, Data Collection and Organization
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Statistical questions
- Data sources and collection methods
- Data organization (frequency tables with discrete data grouped and ungrouped)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Formulate statistical questions about qualitative and quantitative variables
- Define data to collect, select source and collection method
- Collect data through collection methods, using credible internet sites
- Group discrete data into classes when necessary for organization and visualization
- Use frequency tables to organize data (including table legends)

### Graphical Representations
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Box plots (extremes and quartiles diagram)
- Critical analysis of graphs

**Learning Objectives:**
- Represent data through box plots, including source, title and legend
- Interpret influence of data alteration on corresponding box plot configuration
- Decide which graphical representation(s) to adopt for data sets
- Analyze and compare different graphical representations from secondary sources
- Discuss adequacy and conclude critically about eventual graphic manipulation effects

### Data Analysis
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Data summary (quartiles, interquartile range)
- Interpretation and conclusion

**Learning Objectives:**
- Relate 2nd quartile with median
- Interpret quartile meaning and calculate their value through different strategies
- Understand interquartile range meaning and calculate it
- Recognize interquartile range as data dispersion measure
- Identify appropriate summary measures for data based on their nature
- Analyze critically appropriate summary measures for data
- Read, interpret and discuss data distributions, highlighting relevant aspects critically
- Draw conclusions, make fundamental decisions and raise new questions

### Study Communication and Dissemination
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Target audience and resources for study dissemination
- Critical analysis of communication

**Learning Objectives:**
- Decide to whom to disseminate completed study and elaborate different communication resources
- Disseminate study, telling the story behind data and raising emerging questions for future studies
- Analyze critically communication of statistical studies in media

**Key Skills:**
- Complete statistical study cycle execution
- Critical graphical analysis
- Statistical literacy development
- Effective communication of results

### Probabilities
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Random experience
- Results space or sample space
- Events
- Probability tables
- Frequentist probability

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize characteristics of random experience
- Recognize set of possible results as results space or sample space
- Recognize and give examples of certain and impossible events
- Designate event elements as "favorable results"
- Interpret events as sets using correct terminology
- Identify events as subsets of sample space
- Identify possible results as elementary events and understand probability sum equals 1
- Construct probability tables associated with random experiences
- Estimate event probability using relative frequency
- Estimate event probability (theoretical)

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Conduct complete statistical studies
- Create and interpret appropriate graphical representations
- Calculate and interpret summary measures
- Apply probability concepts to solve problems
- Communicate statistical findings effectively

## Geometry and Measurement

### Plane Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Pythagorean theorem
- Regular polygon area

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain Pythagorean theorem in own words
- Apply Pythagorean theorem
- Understand Pythagorean theorem demonstration
- Interpret situations with Pythagorean theorem and solve problems requiring its use
- Calculate regular polygon area measure

**Key Skills:**
- Geometric theorem application
- Problem solving in mathematical and real contexts
- Area calculation using decomposition methods

### Operations with Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Vectors and vector addition
- Translation associated with vector
- Glide reflection
- Figure symmetry

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand vector meaning
- Add vectors
- Construct figure image through translation and glide reflection
- Relate composition of translations with vector addition
- Construct simple friezes
- Identify symmetries, including translation and glide reflection symmetries
- Interpret and model real-world situations involving symmetry

### Space Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Cylinder and cone nets
- Surface area of right prisms, regular pyramids, cylinders, cones
- Volume of right prisms, regular pyramids, cones and spheres

**Learning Objectives:**
- Construct net of given cylinder and vice versa
- Construct net of given cone and vice versa
- Solve surface area problems through composition or decomposition
- Solve solid volume problems through composition or decomposition

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Apply Pythagorean theorem in various contexts
- Calculate areas and volumes using appropriate formulas
- Construct and analyze geometric transformations
- Solve problems involving 2D and 3D geometry
- Connect geometric concepts to real-world applications

## Assessment Framework

**Mathematical Knowledge**: Students must demonstrate fluent understanding of concepts, procedures and methods across all four themes.

**Mathematical Capacities**: Students must show progressive development in problem-solving, reasoning, computational thinking, communication, representations, and connections.

**General Competencies**: Development of critical thinking, creativity, collaboration, self-regulation, self-confidence, perseverance, initiative, autonomy, and valuing mathematical knowledge.

**Integration Requirements**: Students must demonstrate ability to connect mathematical themes internally and apply mathematics to other subject areas and real-world contexts.

**Technology Use**: Appropriate use of calculators, dynamic geometry software, programming environments, and internet resources for mathematical exploration and problem-solving.
