### Content/Summary

#### Set of Natural Numbers

The set of natural numbers is represented by ℕ and consists of {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, ...}. This set is infinite.

#### Divisibility, Multiples and Divisors

A number is divisible by another when the division produces a remainder of zero. If divisible, the first number is a multiple; the second is a divisor.

**Example:** "10 : 2 = 5 (remainder 0)" means that:
- 10 is divisible by 2
- 10 is a multiple of 2
- 2 is a divisor of 10

#### Divisibility Criteria

Numbers are divisible by:
- **2**: if it is even (ends in 0, 2, 4, 6, 8)
- **3**: if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 3
- **4**: if it ends in 00 or the last two digits form a multiple of 4
- **5**: if it ends in 0 or 5
- **6**: if it is divisible by both 2 and 3
- **9**: if the sum of its digits is a multiple of 9
- **10**: if it ends in 0

#### How to Find Multiples and Divisors

**Multiples:** Multiply the number by 0, 1, 2, 3...
- Example: Multiples of 4 = {0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...}

**Divisors:** Test divisions from 1 up to the number itself, recording those that give remainder 0.
- Example: Divisors of 10 = {1, 2, 5, 10}

#### Properties of Divisors

1. In a product, a divisor of a factor is a divisor of the product
2. If a number divides two others, it divides their sum and difference
3. In division: if a number divides the divisor and the remainder, it divides the dividend
4. In division: if a number divides the dividend and the divisor, it divides the remainder

---

### PRIME AND COMPOSITE NUMBERS

#### Definitions

- **Prime Number:** has exactly two divisors (1 and itself)
  - Example: "5 is prime" - has only the divisors 1 and 5

- **Composite Number:** has more than two divisors
  - Example: "6 is composite" - has the divisors 1, 2, 3, 6

- **Important Note:** The number 1 is neither prime nor composite

#### Identifying Prime Numbers

To determine if a number is prime:
1. List all of its divisors
2. Verify if it has exactly two divisors (1 and itself)
3. If it has more than two divisors, it is composite

---

### RELATIVELY PRIME NUMBERS

#### Definition

Two numbers are relatively prime (primos entre si) when their greatest common divisor (GCD) equals 1.

#### Example

"Divisors of 21 = {1, 3, 7, 21}, Divisors of 8 = {1, 2, 4, 8}, GCD(8, 21) = 1"

Conclusion: 8 and 21 are relatively prime.

#### Importance

- Dividing two numbers by their GCD results in relatively prime numbers
- A fraction with numerator and denominator that are relatively prime is irreducible

---

### Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The smallest number (excluding 0) that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

**Example:**
- Multiples of 10 = {0, 10, 20, 30, 40...}
- Multiples of 4 = {0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...}
- LCM(4, 10) = 20

---

### Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

The largest number that divides two or more numbers.

**Example:**
- Divisors of 10 = {1, 2, 5, 10}
- Divisors of 4 = {1, 2, 4}
- GCD(4, 10) = 2

#### Euclid's Algorithm

Method for calculating the GCD:
1. Divide the larger by the smaller number
2. Divide the previous divisor by the previous remainder
3. Repeat until the remainder is 0
4. The last divisor is the GCD

**Example:** GCD(30, 50):
- 50 : 30 = 1 (remainder 20)
- 30 : 20 = 1 (remainder 10)
- 20 : 10 = 2 (remainder 0)
- **Result: GCD = 10**

---

### Examples and Practical Applications

#### Maximum Division Problems (GCD)

Find the largest number that can divide items into equal groups.

**Example:**
24 daisies, 16 tulips, 20 roses → GCD(24, 16, 20) = 4 bouquets
Each bouquet contains: 6 daisies, 4 tulips, and 5 roses

#### Simultaneous Events Problems (LCM)

"In a village, the Octopus Festival takes place every 6 years, and the Sardine Festival takes place every 8 years."

Using LCM(6, 8) = 24:
If both occurred in 2010, they will coincide again in 2034.

---

### Relationship Between LCM and GCD

**Important formula:**
"The product of two numbers equals the product of their greatest common divisor and their least common multiple"

**a × b = GCD(a, b) × LCM(a, b)**

### Fundamental Concepts

#### Prime and Composite Numbers

**Composite numbers:** "have more than two divisors"

**Prime numbers:** "have only two divisors (1 and themselves)"

**Important note:** The number 1 is not classified as composite or prime, as it has only one divisor.

#### Practical Classification Example
- Divisors of 10 = {1, 2, 5, 10} → **composite** number
- Divisors of 11 = {1, 11} → **prime** number

---

### Sieve of Eratosthenes

Practical method for finding prime numbers. Procedure to discover primes up to 100:

1. Write a table with numbers from 1 to 100
2. Cross out 1
3. Cross out multiples of 2 greater than 2
4. Cross out multiples of 3 greater than 3
5. Cross out multiples of 5 greater than 5
6. Cross out multiples of 7 greater than 7

**Result:** The numbers not crossed out are primes up to 100.

---

### Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

**Definition:** "Given a natural number greater than 1, there exists a unique increasing sequence of prime numbers whose product equals that number."

**Example:** 90 = 2 × 3 × 3 × 5 = 2 × 3² × 5

---

### Prime Factorization

#### Practical Method

1. Divide the number by the smallest prime divisor
2. Successively divide the quotients by the smallest prime divisors until obtaining quotient 1
3. The initial number is the product of the divisors used

**Example:** 630 = 2 × 3 × 3 × 5 × 7 = 2 × 3² × 5 × 7

#### Application: Discovering Divisors of a Number

1. Decompose the number into prime factors
2. Multiply factors among themselves in all possible ways
3. Result includes 1, prime factors, and resulting products

**Example for 90 = 2 × 3 × 3 × 5:**
- Possible products: 6, 9, 10, 15, 18, 30, 45, 90
- **Divisors of 90 = {1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 15, 18, 30, 45, 90}**

---

### Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

#### Method by Prime Factorization

1. Decompose numbers into prime factors
2. GCD = product of **common prime factors with the smallest exponent**

**Example:**
- 630 = 2 × 3² × 5 × 7
- 100 = 2² × 5²
- **GCD(630, 100) = 2 × 5 = 10**

#### Venn Diagram Method

1. Decompose into prime factors
2. Arrange factors in the diagram (common ones in the intersection)
3. GCD = product of factors in the intersection

#### Application: Simplifying Fractions

Divide numerator and denominator by the GCD:

**Example:** 24/36
- 24 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3
- 36 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 3
- GCD(24, 36) = 12
- **24/36 = 2/3**

**Note:** If GCD = 1, numbers are relatively prime and the fraction is already irreducible.

---

### Least Common Multiple (LCM)

#### Method by Prime Factorization

1. Decompose numbers into prime factors
2. LCM = product of **common factors with the largest exponent and non-common factors**

**Example:**
- 630 = 2 × 3² × 5 × 7
- 100 = 2² × 5²
- **LCM(630, 100) = 2² × 5² × 3² × 7 = 6300**

#### Venn Diagram Method

LCM = product of **all** factors arranged in the circles

**Example:** LCM(630, 100) = 2 × 5 × 3 × 3 × 7 × 2 × 5 = 6300
