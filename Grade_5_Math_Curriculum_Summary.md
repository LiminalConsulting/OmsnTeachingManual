# Portuguese Grade 5 Mathematics Curriculum - Essential Summary

## Mathematical Capacities (Transversal Skills)

### Problem Solving
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize and apply problem-solving process stages
- Formulate problems from given situations in diverse contexts
- Apply and adapt diverse problem-solving strategies using technology
- Recognize correctness, difference and efficacy of different strategies

**Key Skills:**
- Strategic thinking (simulation, working backwards, trial and error, simpler problems, particular cases, diagrams)
- Critical evaluation of solutions
- Creative problem formulation

### Mathematical Reasoning
**Learning Objectives:**
- Formulate and test conjectures/generalizations from pattern identification
- Classify objects by their characteristics
- Distinguish between testing and validating a conjecture
- Justify conjectures using progressive symbolic language

**Key Skills:**
- Inductive reasoning and pattern recognition
- Logical argumentation and validation
- Critical analysis of mathematical reasoning

### Computational Thinking
**Learning Objectives:**
- Extract essential information from problems (abstraction)
- Structure problem resolution in stages of lower complexity (decomposition)
- Recognize patterns and regularities in problem-solving processes
- Develop step-by-step procedures (algorithms) using technology
- Debug, test, refine and optimize solutions

**Key Skills:**
- Algorithmic thinking
- Pattern recognition and application
- Systematic error correction and optimization

### Mathematical Communication
**Learning Objectives:**
- Describe thinking about mathematical ideas and processes orally and in writing
- Listen to others, question and discuss ideas in a well-founded manner
- Use multiple representations to demonstrate comprehension and express ideas
- Use symbolic mathematical language and recognize its value for precise communication

**Key Skills:**
- Clear mathematical expression (oral and written)
- Active listening and constructive discussion
- Effective use of mathematical representations

### Mathematical Representations
**Learning Objectives:**
- Read and interpret mathematical ideas expressed through diverse representations
- Use multiple representations (especially verbal language and diagrams)
- Establish connections between different representations of the same mathematical ideas/processes

**Key Skills:**
- Visual representation creation and interpretation
- Fluent transition between representation types
- Connection-making between different mathematical formats

### Mathematical Connections
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize and use connections between mathematical ideas from different themes
- Apply mathematical ideas in solving problems from diverse contexts (other areas, reality, professions)
- Interpret situations mathematically, construct adequate mathematical models
- Identify mathematics presence in external contexts

**Key Skills:**
- Internal mathematical connection recognition
- Real-world application of mathematics
- Mathematical modeling of real situations

## 1. NUMBERS

### Natural Numbers
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Multiples and divisors
- Prime numbers
- Powers (base and natural exponents)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize divisor relationships and identify multiples/divisors
- Understand finite divisors vs infinite multiples
- Identify prime numbers less than 100
- Recognize powers as repeated multiplication products
- Model exponential growth phenomena
- Write numbers like 10, 100, 1000 as powers of 10

**Key Skills:**
- Number relationship analysis
- Prime number identification
- Exponential pattern recognition
- Mathematical modeling of growth phenomena

### Fractions, Decimals and Percentages
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Equivalent fractions
- Percentage relationships
- Comparison and ordering
- Approximation values

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize and determine equivalent fractions through multiplicative relationships
- Relate percentages with fractions of denominator 100
- Compare and order fractions and decimals, representing them on number lines
- Establish relationships between fractions, decimals and percentages
- Determine approximate values (by excess/deficit) to hundredths
- Make roundings in problem-solving contexts

**Key Skills:**
- Fraction equivalence recognition
- Number format conversion (fraction↔decimal↔percentage)
- Number line representation and ordering
- Strategic approximation and rounding

### Operations with Fractions and Decimals
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Addition and subtraction of fractions
- Multiplication between naturals and fractions
- Decimal multiplication and division
- Mental calculation strategies

**Learning Objectives:**
- Add/subtract fractions when one denominator is multiple of another
- Recognize natural×fraction multiplication as successive fraction addition
- Multiply fraction by natural number, giving meaning to fraction as operator
- Perform multiplications and divisions involving decimals and naturals
- Relate division by 0.1, 0.01, 0.001 with multiplication by 10, 100, 1000
- Use fluent mental calculation strategies with fractions and decimals

**Key Skills:**
- Fraction operation procedures
- Decimal calculation fluency
- Mental calculation strategy development
- Reasonableness assessment of operation results

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate procedural fluency with fraction and decimal operations
- Apply mental calculation strategies appropriately
- Justify calculation approaches and verify result reasonableness

## 2. ALGEBRA

### Regularities in Sequences
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Growth sequences
- Formation laws

**Learning Objectives:**
- Justify conjectures involving relationships between sequence term and its order (functional thinking)
- Identify and describe formation laws in natural, pictorial and symbolic language
- Create, complete and continue numerical sequences according to formation law
- Verify if a given number is sequence element, justifying

**Key Skills:**
- Pattern recognition in numerical and geometric sequences
- Mathematical language transition (natural↔pictorial↔symbolic)
- Functional relationship identification
- Sequence continuation and verification

### Numerical and Algebraic Relations
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Algebraic expressions with letters
- Equivalent algebraic expressions

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify properties of set elements or relationships between elements, describing them through words, drawings or algebraic expressions
- Express relationships and simple properties in symbolic language and reciprocally
- Determine algebraic expression value when assigning numerical value to letter
- Identify equivalent algebraic expressions, relating them to their context meaning

**Key Skills:**
- Algebraic thinking development
- Symbolic representation of relationships
- Variable substitution and evaluation
- Equivalence recognition in different contexts

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Create and interpret algebraic expressions
- Demonstrate understanding of variable meaning in different contexts
- Solve problems involving algebraic expressions

## 3. DATA AND PROBABILITIES

### Statistical Questions and Data Organization
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Statistical questions
- Data collection sources and methods
- Questionnaires
- Frequency tables

**Learning Objectives:**
- Formulate questions of student interest about qualitative and discrete quantitative characteristics
- Participate in defining data to collect and decide collection sources (primary/secondary)
- Critically participate in data collection method selection
- Construct simple questionnaires with closed-response questions using technology
- Use absolute and relative frequency tables (percentage) to register and organize data

**Key Skills:**
- Statistical question formulation
- Data collection methodology selection
- Questionnaire design and implementation
- Data organization in tables with appropriate titles

### Graphic Representations
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Circular graphs
- Bar graphs
- Juxtaposed bar graphs
- Critical graph analysis

**Learning Objectives:**
- Represent data through relative frequency circular graphs
- Represent data through relative frequency bar graphs with adequate scales, including source, title and legends
- Represent data sets through juxtaposed bar graphs (absolute and relative frequencies)
- Analyze and compare different graphic representations in media, discussing adequacy and concluding critically about graphic manipulation effects

**Key Skills:**
- Appropriate graphic representation selection
- Graphic construction with proper elements (title, source, legend)
- Critical analysis of media representations
- Graphic manipulation effect identification

### Data Analysis
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Data summary - mean
- Interpretation and conclusion

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify mean as resulting value from equitable distribution of total data (equilibrium point)
- Calculate mean using adequate procedure and understand this measure is sensitive to each data point
- Identify which summary measures are possible to calculate for qualitative and quantitative data
- Read, interpret and discuss data distribution, critically highlighting most relevant aspects
- Draw conclusions, base decisions and pose new questions raised by obtained conclusions

**Key Skills:**
- Mean calculation and interpretation
- Appropriate summary measure selection
- Critical data distribution analysis
- Evidence-based conclusion drawing

### Communication and Dissemination
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Digital posters

**Learning Objectives:**
- Elaborate digital poster supporting oral presentation of conducted study, attending to target audience, telling the story behind data, and posing emerging questions for future studies

**Key Skills:**
- Effective scientific communication
- Digital presentation creation
- Story-telling with data
- Future research question formulation

### Probabilities
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Relative frequency for probability estimation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize probability expresses degree of conviction in event realization
- Recognize probability assumes value between 0% and 100%
- Estimate event probabilities using relative frequency
- Conjecture about conviction degree in characteristic occurrence in a group based on information from different groups
- Use probabilities to know and understand surrounding world, recognizing mathematics utility and power in uncertain event prediction

**Key Skills:**
- Probability concept understanding as conviction degree
- Relative frequency calculation and interpretation
- Probability estimation from data
- Real-world probability application

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate complete statistical investigation process
- Create and interpret appropriate graphic representations
- Calculate and interpret mean appropriately
- Estimate probabilities using relative frequency
- Communicate statistical findings effectively

## 4. GEOMETRY AND MEASUREMENT

### Plane Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Lines, rays and line segments
- Relative position of lines
- Angle amplitude
- Angle construction
- Triangle classification
- Triangle construction
- Triangle congruence criteria
- Plane figure equivalence
- Parallelogram area
- Triangle area

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish line from ray and line segment
- Identify relative position of parallel and concurrent lines (perpendicular or oblique)
- Understand angle amplitude can be measured knowing unit measure degree
- Measure angle amplitude using protractor with degree approximation and classify it
- Make amplitude estimates by comparison with reference angles (45°, 90°, 180°)
- Construct angles with given amplitude measure
- Classify triangles by sides and angles
- Describe relationships between triangle sides and angles
- Construct triangles and understand construction possibility cases
- Recognize triangle congruence criteria and use them in construction and problem-solving
- Understand equivalent figures meaning
- Generalize and justify parallelogram area calculation expression
- Generalize and justify triangle area calculation expression
- Identify parallelogram and triangle heights

**Key Skills:**
- Geometric construction using tools (ruler, compass, protractor)
- Angle measurement and estimation
- Triangle classification and property recognition
- Area calculation through decomposition and composition
- Geometric reasoning and justification

### Space Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Polyhedra properties
- Polyhedra nets

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify parallel and perpendicular face pairs in prisms
- Explain hierarchical classification between right prisms, rectangular parallelepipeds and cubes
- Formulate and test conjectures identifying regularities in polyhedra classes involving elements
- Justify relationships between polyhedra class elements using spatial organization
- Identify and construct polyhedra from their nets, establishing relationships between net elements and polyhedron
- Construct and recognize different nets for same polyhedron

**Key Skills:**
- 3D spatial visualization
- Polyhedra classification and relationship recognition
- Net construction and interpretation
- Geometric pattern recognition in 3D shapes

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate accurate use of geometric instruments
- Classify and construct triangles appropriately
- Calculate areas using appropriate formulas and methods
- Recognize and apply triangle congruence criteria
- Construct and interpret polyhedra nets correctly
- Justify geometric relationships and properties
