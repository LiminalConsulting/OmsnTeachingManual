### Ciências Naturais 8º ano | A Terra como um sistema capaz de gerar vida

This educational resource covers Earth as a system capable of generating life, tailored for 8th-grade Natural Sciences students in Portugal, aligned with curriculum standards.

### Earth's Subsystems

The page identifies four interconnected components:
- **Geosfera** (solid rocky layer)
- **Hidrosfera** (water in solid/liquid states)
- **Atmosfera** (surrounding layer)
- **Biosfera** (living organisms)

### System Interactions

Examples include:
- Photosynthetic organisms removing carbon dioxide while releasing oxygen
- Water evaporation cycles
- Volcanic gas emissions
- Erosive water action on rocks

### Origin of Life Theories

Four main hypotheses are presented:

1. **Creationism** - divine creation
2. **Cosmozoic Hypothesis** - life from space
3. **Spontaneism** - spontaneous generation
4. **Evolutionary Hypothesis by Oparin** - organic molecules from atmospheric compounds via electrical discharges

### Urey-Miller Experiment

"in 1953, Harold Urey and Stanley Miller constructed a device that intended to recreate conditions" when life emerged, successfully creating organic compounds without living organisms.

### Current Theory

Life emerged from "protovida" (organic molecules) formed from methane and ammonia in hydrothermal vents on ocean floors, followed by evolutionary explosion 600 million years ago.

### Soil Formation & Importance

Soils develop through slow rock weathering, organized in horizons (O, A, B, C layers), serving as:
- Habitat
- Support structure
- Water retention
- Nutrient fixation

### Subsystem Roles

Each subsystem maintains life through specific functions:
- **Geosfera** provides substrate and magnetic protection
- **Hidrosfera** offers habitat and water reserves
- **Atmosfera** supplies essential gases and radiation protection
- **Biosfera** provides organic matter
