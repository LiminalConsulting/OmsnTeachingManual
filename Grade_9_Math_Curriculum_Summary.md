# Grade 9 Mathematics Curriculum - Essential Content Summary

## Mathematical Capacities (Transversal)

### Problem Solving
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize and apply stages of problem-solving process
  - Formulate problems from given situations in various contexts
  - Apply and adapt diverse problem-solving strategies using technology
  - Recognize correctness, difference, and efficiency of different strategies

### Mathematical Reasoning
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Formulate and test conjectures/generalizations from pattern identification
  - Classify objects based on their characteristics
  - Distinguish between testing and validating conjectures
  - Justify conjectures using progressive symbolic language
  - Recognize correctness and adequacy of different justification forms

### Computational Thinking
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Extract essential information from problems (abstraction)
  - Structure problem resolution in less complex stages (decomposition)
  - Recognize patterns and apply them to similar problems
  - Develop step-by-step procedures (algorithms) using technology
  - Debug, test, refine and optimize solutions

### Mathematical Communication
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Describe thinking about mathematical ideas and processes orally and in writing
  - Listen to others, question and discuss ideas in a reasoned way
  - Use multiple representations to demonstrate understanding and express ideas
  - Establish connections between different representations

### Mathematical Connections
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize connections between mathematical ideas from different themes
  - Apply mathematical ideas to solve problems in diverse contexts
  - Interpret situations mathematically, construct adequate models
  - Identify mathematics presence in external contexts

## Numbers

### Real Numbers

#### Meaning of Real Numbers
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize existence of points on number line that don't represent rational numbers
  - Know irrational numbers as numbers represented by infinite non-periodic decimals
  - Recognize ℝ as the set of real numbers
  - Conjecture, generalize and justify properties of real numbers

#### Representation and Ordering on Real Line
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Correspond each point on number line with a real number and vice versa
  - Compare and order real numbers using symbols "<", "≤", ">" or "≥"
  - Identify, describe and represent real number intervals on number line
  - Establish relationships between intervals using symbols ⊂, ⊃ and =
  - Represent intersection and union of number intervals and various sets

#### Operations with Real Numbers
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Add, subtract and multiply rational with irrational numbers in simple cases
  - Recognize that properties of operations with rational numbers extend to real numbers
  - Use mental calculation strategies fluently with real numbers
  - Determine approximate values by excess or deficit of sums and products
  - Operate with approximate values and analyze error associated with rounding

## Algebra

### Algebraic Expressions, Equations and Inequalities

#### Notable Cases of Binomial Multiplication & Polynomial Factorization
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Apply distributive property of multiplication over addition of monomials
  - Generalize notable cases from prior knowledge of polynomial operations
  - Factor polynomials using distributive property or notable cases

#### Second Degree Equations
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize second degree equations with one unknown
  - Translate situations into second degree equations and vice versa
  - Know and apply zero product law
  - Solve complete second degree equations using notable cases
  - Recognize possible determined and impossible equations
  - Solve problems involving second degree equations in various contexts

#### First Degree Inequalities
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize and translate situations using first degree inequalities
  - Solve first degree inequalities and related problems

### Functions

#### Quadratic Functions f(x) = ax², a ∈ ℝ\{0}
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize quadratic function expression as second degree polynomial
  - Identify characteristics of graph family f(x) = ax²
  - Distinguish between quadratic and linear function graphs
  - Recognize quadratic functions in real world

#### Inverse Proportionality Function
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Interpret and solve inverse proportionality problems
  - Identify inversely proportional variables and calculate proportionality constant
  - Represent and recognize inverse proportionality through multiple representations
  - Solve problems using inverse proportionality functions
  - Interpret and model situations from other areas involving inverse proportionality

## Data and Probability

### Statistical Questions, Data Collection and Organization

#### Statistical Questions
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Formulate statistical questions about qualitative and quantitative variables

#### Data Source and Collection Methods
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Define data to collect, select source and collection method
  - Collect data through collection methods, including credible internet sites

#### Continuous Data Grouping in Classes
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Construct classes for continuous data or work with pre-grouped continuous data

#### Data Organization
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Use frequency tables to organize data (with legend)

### Graphic Representations

#### Histogram
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Represent continuous data grouped in classes through histograms
  - Recognize histograms can represent discrete data grouped in classes
  - Recognize same data set can be represented by different histograms

#### Parallel Box-and-Whisker Plots
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Represent data through parallel box-and-whisker plots

#### Critical Analysis of Graphics
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Decide on appropriate graphic representation(s) for data sets
  - Analyze and compare different graphic representations from secondary sources
  - Discuss adequacy and critically conclude about graphic manipulation effects

### Data Analysis

#### Data Summary
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Interpret location and dispersion measures, relating them to histogram and box-plot representations

#### Interpretation and Conclusion
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Critically analyze appropriate summary measures for data
  - Read, interpret and discuss data distributions, highlighting relevant aspects
  - Draw conclusions, justify decisions and pose new questions

### Study Communication and Dissemination

#### Target Audience and Communication Resources
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Decide study dissemination audience and elaborate different communication resources
  - Disseminate study telling the story behind data and raising emerging questions

#### Critical Communication Analysis
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Critically analyze communication of statistical studies in media

### Probabilities

#### Ways to Represent Events
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Represent events through Venn diagrams, tree diagrams and tables

#### Operations with Events
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Assign meaning to union and intersection of events
  - Recognize and give examples of complementary and contrary events
  - Recognize disjoint or mutually exclusive events

#### Laplace Rule
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Calculate probabilities using Laplace rule in applicable situations

#### Probability of Union of Disjoint Events
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Calculate probability of union of disjoint events

## Geometry and Measurement

### Plane Figures

#### Central Angle and Inscribed Angle in Circumference
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Recognize central and inscribed angles in circumference
  - Relate amplitude of central angle with arc and corresponding chord measure
  - Relate amplitude of inscribed angle with associated arc
  - Relate inscribed angle amplitude with central angle with same associated arc
  - Recognize circumference tangent as perpendicular to radius at tangency point
  - Solve problems involving circumferences applying studied relations
  - Reason mathematically relating quadrilateral classification and inscribed quadrilaterals

#### Constructions and Geometric Places
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Identify circumference, circle, angle bisector and segment perpendicular bisector as geometric places
  - Construct regular polygons inscribed in circumference
  - Perform constructions in dynamic geometry environments mobilizing geometric places, regular polygons, angle relations and isometries

#### Trigonometric Ratios in Right Triangle
- **Learning Objectives:**
  - Identify sine, cosine and tangent of acute angle
  - Distinguish trigonometric ratios through simple situation confrontation
  - Solve problems using trigonometric ratios

## Assessment Requirements

Students must demonstrate:
- **Knowledge Application**: Fluent use of mathematical concepts in diverse situations
- **Problem-Solving Competence**: Strategic thinking and solution validation
- **Mathematical Reasoning**: Conjecture formation, pattern recognition, and logical justification
- **Communication Skills**: Clear expression of mathematical thinking orally and in writing
- **Representation Use**: Effective use of multiple mathematical representations
- **Connection Making**: Links between mathematical themes and real-world applications
- **Computational Thinking**: Algorithm development and systematic problem decomposition
- **Critical Analysis**: Evaluation of mathematical arguments and real-world applications

---

## Key Skills Required

- Mental calculation with real numbers
- Algebraic manipulation and equation solving
- Function analysis and graphical interpretation
- Statistical data collection, organization and analysis
- Probability calculation using various methods
- Geometric reasoning with circles and triangles
- Technology use for mathematical exploration
- Mathematical modeling of real situations
- Critical evaluation of mathematical information
- Collaborative problem-solving and communication
