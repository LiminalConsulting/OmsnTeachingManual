# Grade 7 Mathematics Curriculum Summary - Portugal

## Mathematical Capacities (Transversal Skills)

### Problem Solving
- **Process**: Recognize and apply problem-solving stages (interpret, strategize, execute, evaluate)
- **Strategy Formulation**: Apply diverse problem-solving strategies in various contexts using technology
- **Problem Creation**: Formulate problems from given situations in mathematical and non-mathematical contexts

### Mathematical Reasoning
- **Conjecturing & Generalizing**: Formulate and test conjectures from pattern identification, using technology
- **Classification**: Classify objects by mathematical characteristics
- **Justification**: Distinguish testing vs. validating conjectures; justify using progressive symbolic language
- **Critical Analysis**: Recognize correctness and adequacy of different justification forms

### Computational Thinking
- **Abstraction**: Extract essential information from problems
- **Decomposition**: Structure problem-solving into simpler steps
- **Pattern Recognition**: Identify patterns and apply to similar problems
- **Algorithm Development**: Develop step-by-step procedures using technology
- **Debugging**: Find and correct errors, test and optimize solutions

### Mathematical Communication
- **Expression**: Describe mathematical thinking orally and in writing
- **Discussion**: Listen, question, discuss ideas fundamentally with reasoned arguments

### Mathematical Representations
- **Multiple Representations**: Use diverse representations (verbal, visual, symbolic) to demonstrate understanding
- **Representation Connections**: Establish relationships between different representations
- **Symbolic Language**: Use mathematical symbolic language recognizing its communication value

### Mathematical Connections
- **Internal Connections**: Recognize connections between different mathematical topics
- **External Connections**: Apply mathematical ideas to diverse contexts using technology
- **Mathematical Models**: Interpret real-world situations, construct adequate models, recognize mathematics in reality

## Numbers

### Integer Numbers
- **Meaning & Representation**: Recognize positive/negative integers, represent on number line
- **Absolute Value & Opposite**: Understand absolute value as distance, recognize opposites
- **Ordering**: Compare and order integers; recognize ℤ and its relationship to ℕ
- **Operations**: Add/subtract integers using properties; recognize subtraction as addition of opposite
- **Numerical Expressions**: Write, simplify and calculate expressions with parentheses
- **Problem Solving**: Solve integer problems in diverse contexts with mathematical reasoning

### Rational Numbers
- **Meaning**: Recognize positive/negative rational numbers in various contexts
- **Representation & Ordering**: Represent on number line, compare and order rationals
- **Number Sets**: Recognize ℚ as set of rationals; understand ℤ ⊆ ℚ relationship
- **Operations**: Add/subtract rationals using properties and mental calculation strategies
- **Percentages**: Solve percentage problems, calculate percentages from whole and vice-versa
- **Scientific Notation**: Represent/compare positive rationals in scientific notation; operate with simple cases

## Algebra

### Patterns, Sequences and Successions
- **Formation Laws**: Recognize regularities, determine formation laws in natural/symbolic language
- **Term Determination**: Find terms of various orders when formation law is known
- **Multiple Representations**: Compare and establish connections between sequence representations

### Algebraic Expressions and Equations
- **Equation Meaning**: Recognize equations, distinguish unknown terms from independent terms
- **Translation**: Translate situations to/from 1st degree equations
- **Equation Solving**: Solve 1st degree equations (without parentheses/denominators) using inverse operations
- **Equivalence**: Justify equation equivalence
- **Problem Solving**: Solve problems involving 1st degree equations, analyzing solution adequacy

### Functions
- **Function Meaning**: Interpret function as univocal correspondence between sets
- **Representations**: Recognize different function representations; model mathematical/real-life situations
- **Situation Description**: Describe relationships between variables from given graphs
- **External Connections**: Recognize functions in other disciplines, establish mathematical connections
- **Direct Proportionality**: Solve problems involving direct proportional relationships; express as functions; represent through graphs/tables; transition between representations

## Data and Probability

### Statistical Questions, Data Collection and Organization
- **Statistical Questions**: Formulate statistical questions about qualitative/quantitative variables
- **Variable Classification**: Classify variables by nature (qualitative: nominal vs ordinal; quantitative: discrete vs continuous)
- **Population vs Sample**: Distinguish population from sample; plan representative sample selection
- **Data Sources**: Define data collection methods, select sources, collect and clean data
- **Discrete Data Grouping**: Identify when discrete data grouping in classes is necessary; construct equal amplitude classes
- **Frequency Tables**: Use frequency tables to organize data in classes (including table titles)

### Graphic Representations
- **Line Graphs**: Represent bivariate data (with time variable) through line graphs with source, title and legend
- **Overlapping Bar Charts**: Represent two data sets through overlapping bar charts with source, title and legend
- **Critical Analysis**: Decide on appropriate graphic representations; analyze and compare different graphics; discuss adequacy and conclude on manipulation effects

### Data Analysis
- **Median & Range**: Recognize and calculate median as central location measure and range as dispersion measure
- **Measure Differences**: Identify differences between central location vs dispersion measures
- **Grouped vs Ungrouped**: Recognize differences in summary measures from ungrouped vs grouped data
- **Critical Analysis**: Analyze which summary measures are appropriate; interpret data distributions
- **Conclusions**: Draw conclusions, support decisions, raise new questions from obtained conclusions

### Communication and Study Dissemination
- **Target Audience**: Decide study dissemination audience and create different communication resources
- **Study Sharing**: Share study telling the story behind data and raising emerging questions
- **Critical Communication Analysis**: Critically analyze statistical study communication in media

### Probability
- **Composite Events**: Recognize probability of events with multiple outcomes equals sum of constituent outcome probabilities

## Geometry and Measurement

### Plane Figures
- **Polygon Angles**: Identify internal/external angles of convex polygons
- **Angle Sums**: Generalize and justify sum formulas for internal/external angle measures
- **Alternate & Opposite Angles**: Recognize equality of alternate internal angles in parallel lines intersected by secant; recognize vertically opposite angle equality
- **Diagonal Properties**: Identify quadrilateral diagonals; describe and apply diagonal properties
- **Hierarchical Classification**: Explain hierarchical quadrilateral classification including trapezoid and kite cases
- **Area Formulas**: Generalize and justify area formulas for trapezoid, rhombus and kite using other figures

### Operations with Figures
- **Similar Figures**: Recognize similar figures as same-shaped figures obtained by enlargement/reduction
- **Similarity Identification**: Identify similar polygons and similarity ratio; construct image by homothety
- **Map Scales**: Recognize similarity in different-scale maps, establishing connections with other knowledge areas
- **Triangle Similarity Criteria**: Identify triangle similarity criteria; recognize inappropriate applications
- **Area & Perimeter Relations**: Know ratios between perimeters and areas of similar figures; apply in concrete situations

### Space Figures
- **Regular Polyhedra**: Distinguish regular vs irregular polyhedra; construct 3D models and some nets
- **Polyhedra Visualization**: Visualize polyhedra and their nets
- **Existence Proof**: Identify existing regular polyhedra and justify non-existence of others
- **Euler's Formula**: Establish relationships between face, edge and vertex numbers; infer Euler's formula from polyhedra analysis

## Assessment Requirements

Students must demonstrate:
- **Problem-solving competency** across all mathematical domains
- **Mathematical reasoning** through conjecture, generalization and justification
- **Communication skills** in mathematical language (oral and written)
- **Representation fluency** across multiple formats
- **Connection-making** within mathematics and with external contexts
- **Computational thinking** including algorithm development and debugging
- **Critical analysis** of mathematical information and solutions
- **Collaborative work** and autonomous learning capabilities
