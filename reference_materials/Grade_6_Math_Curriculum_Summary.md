# Portuguese Mathematics Curriculum - Grade 6
## Essential Learning Content Summary

---

## MATHEMATICAL CAPACITIES (Transversal)

### Problem Solving
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize and apply problem-solving process stages
- Formulate problems from given situations (mathematical and non-mathematical contexts)
- Apply diverse problem-solving strategies using technology
- Recognize correctness, differences, and efficiency of different strategies

**Key Skills:**
- Systematic application of problem-solving steps (interpret, select strategy, execute, evaluate)
- Creative strategy adaptation
- Critical analysis of solution methods

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate perseverance in mathematical work
- Show ability to use multiple strategies
- Justify strategy selection and effectiveness

### Mathematical Reasoning
**Learning Objectives:**
- Formulate and test conjectures/generalizations from pattern identification
- Classify objects by their characteristics
- Distinguish between testing and validating conjectures
- Justify conjectures using progressive symbolic language

**Key Skills:**
- Pattern recognition and generalization
- Logical argumentation
- Use of examples, counterexamples, and logical coherence

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Provide valid mathematical justifications
- Demonstrate critical thinking in analyzing reasoning

### Computational Thinking
**Learning Objectives:**
- Extract essential information from problems (abstraction)
- Structure problem-solving in stages of reduced complexity (decomposition)
- Recognize patterns and apply to similar problems
- Develop step-by-step procedures (algorithms)
- Debug, test, refine, and optimize solutions

**Key Skills:**
- Problem decomposition
- Algorithm development
- Error detection and correction
- Process optimization

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate systematic problem-solving approach
- Show ability to transfer patterns across contexts

### Mathematical Communication
**Learning Objectives:**
- Describe mathematical thinking orally and in writing
- Listen, question, and discuss ideas in a well-founded manner
- Use multiple representations for reasoning and communication
- Use mathematical symbolic language progressively

**Key Skills:**
- Clear mathematical expression
- Active listening and constructive discussion
- Representation translation and connection

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Communicate mathematical ideas clearly
- Engage in meaningful mathematical dialogue

### Mathematical Representations
**Learning Objectives:**
- Read and interpret mathematical ideas expressed through diverse representations
- Use multiple representations to demonstrate understanding and express processes
- Establish connections between different representations
- Recognize value of symbolic mathematical language

**Key Skills:**
- Multi-representational fluency
- Representation translation
- Strategic representation selection

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate understanding across multiple representations
- Justify representation choices

### Mathematical Connections
**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize internal connections between mathematical themes
- Apply mathematical ideas to problems in diverse contexts
- Interpret situations mathematically and construct appropriate models
- Identify mathematics presence in external contexts

**Key Skills:**
- Cross-topic mathematical integration
- Mathematical modeling
- Real-world application

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate mathematical coherence understanding
- Apply mathematics to solve real-world problems

## NUMBERS

### Natural Numbers
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Prime factorization
- Least common multiple (LCM) and greatest common divisor (GCD)
- Multiplication and division of powers

**Learning Objectives:**
- Represent natural numbers as products of prime factors
- Calculate LCM and GCD using multiple methods
- Select most efficient calculation methods
- Apply power operation rules

**Key Skills:**
- Systematic decomposition methods
- Method selection and justification
- Problem-solving with LCM/GCD applications

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate understanding of prime factorization uniqueness
- Solve problems requiring LCM/GCD in various contexts

### Fractions
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Irreducible fractions
- Addition and subtraction of fractions
- Multiplication and division of fractions
- Powers of type (a/b)ⁿ
- Numerical expressions with fractions

**Learning Objectives:**
- Determine equivalent irreducible fractions
- Perform all four operations with fractions
- Understand fractions as measurements and ratios
- Calculate powers with fractional bases
- Use and create numerical expressions

**Key Skills:**
- Fraction operation fluency
- Geometric representation of fraction operations
- Mental calculation with fractions using properties

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Solve problems involving fraction operations
- Demonstrate understanding of fraction as measure and ratio concepts

## ALGEBRA

### Sequences and Patterns
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Formation laws of decreasing sequences
- Sequence creation and continuation

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize relationships between consecutive terms
- Identify and describe formation laws in natural or symbolic language
- Create, complete, and continue sequences
- Verify if given numbers belong to sequences

**Key Skills:**
- Pattern recognition in decreasing sequences
- Law formulation and verification
- Sequence analysis involving powers and fractions

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate understanding of sequence patterns
- Solve problems involving sequence regularities

### Direct Proportionality
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Direct proportional relationships
- Ratio, proportion, and proportionality constant
- Numerical and algebraic relations

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize multiplicative nature of direct proportionality
- Distinguish proportional from non-proportional relationships
- Understand fractions as ratios between parts
- Determine quantities using proportional reasoning
- Express proportionality through algebraic expressions

**Key Skills:**
- Proportional reasoning across multiple representations
- Critical analysis of pseudo-proportional problems
- Flexible problem-solving approaches

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Solve proportionality problems in diverse contexts
- Demonstrate understanding of ratio and proportion concepts

## DATA AND PROBABILITIES

### Statistical Questions and Data Organization
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Continuous quantitative variables
- Data sources and collection methods
- Class organization for continuous data
- Frequency tables with class organization

**Learning Objectives:**
- Formulate questions about continuous quantitative characteristics
- Participate in defining data collection processes
- Recognize need for class grouping with continuous data
- Construct equal-amplitude classes
- Use frequency tables with appropriate titles

**Key Skills:**
- Statistical investigation planning
- Data organization and class construction
- Critical analysis of data organization choices

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Conduct simple statistical studies
- Demonstrate understanding of continuous data characteristics

### Graphical Representations
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Line graphs for temporal evolution
- Histograms for continuous data
- Critical analysis of graphs

**Learning Objectives:**
- Represent temporal data through line graphs
- Represent continuous data through histograms
- Critically analyze graphical representations in media
- Decide on appropriate graphical representations

**Key Skills:**
- Appropriate graph selection
- Critical evaluation of graph manipulations
- Effective visual communication

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Create appropriate graphical representations
- Analyze and critique graphs from various sources

### Data Analysis
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Modal class identification
- Data interpretation and conclusion drawing

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize modal class as highest frequency class
- Critically analyze appropriate summary measures
- Read, interpret, and discuss data distribution
- Draw conclusions and pose new questions

**Key Skills:**
- Statistical reasoning
- Critical data interpretation
- Conclusion formulation

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate statistical literacy
- Make informed decisions based on data analysis

### Communication and Dissemination
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Statistical reports
- Digital infographics

**Learning Objectives:**
- Communicate studies through reports telling data stories
- Create rigorous, effective, non-misleading digital infographics

**Key Skills:**
- Clear statistical communication
- Visual design for data presentation
- Audience-appropriate communication

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Produce effective statistical communications
- Demonstrate understanding of ethical data presentation

### Probabilities
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Equiprobable events
- Probability comparison

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify situations with equal probability outcomes
- Recognize equal probabilities for equiprobable events

**Key Skills:**
- Probabilistic reasoning
- Critical analysis of random situations

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Distinguish between equiprobable and non-equiprobable situations
- Demonstrate understanding of probability concepts

## GEOMETRY AND MEASUREMENT

### Plane Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Concave and convex polygons
- Regular and irregular polygons
- Circle perimeter and area
- Complementary and supplementary angles
- Sum of internal and external angles of triangles

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish concave from convex polygons
- Distinguish regular from irregular polygons
- Recognize direct proportionality between perimeter and diameter (π)
- Know circle area expression
- Classify complementary and supplementary angles
- Conjecture about triangle angle sums

**Key Skills:**
- Geometric classification and reasoning
- Experimental investigation of geometric properties
- Problem-solving with geometric measurements

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Solve problems involving polygons and circles
- Demonstrate understanding of angle relationships

### Spatial Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Volume concept and measurement
- Volume units (conventional and non-conventional)
- Volume of parallelepipeds, cubes, and cylinders

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand volume concept
- Measure volume using appropriate units
- Recognize correspondence between cubic decimeter and liter
- Generalize volume expressions through structured counting
- Know cylinder volume expression

**Key Skills:**
- Three-dimensional visualization
- Volume calculation and estimation
- Unit conversion understanding

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Solve problems involving volume calculations
- Demonstrate understanding of volume as spatial measurement

### Operations with Figures
**Topics & Subtopics:**
- Rotation image construction
- Rotation and reflection symmetries of rosettes

**Learning Objectives:**
- Construct point and polygon images by rotation
- Analyze rosette symmetries and construction methods
- Relate reflection axes number to minimum rotation angle
- Construct rosettes through successive rotations

**Key Skills:**
- Geometric construction with tools and dynamic geometry
- Symmetry analysis and creation
- Programming of geometric constructions

**Assessment Requirements:**
- Demonstrate understanding of geometric transformations
- Create and analyze symmetric figures

## ASSESSMENT APPROACH

**Formative Assessment:**
- Continuous evaluation integrated into learning process
- Error viewed as part of learning
- Student self-regulation development
- Criteria negotiation and appropriation

**Summative Assessment:**
- Diverse instruments matching learning objectives
- Knowledge, capacity, and attitude evaluation
- Problem-solving and reasoning assessment
- Mathematical communication evaluation

**Key Assessment Principles:**
- Coherence with curriculum components
- Multiple representation usage
- Real-world application demonstration
- Mathematical reasoning and justification
